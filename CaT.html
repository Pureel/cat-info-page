What Cats Are

Cats (Felis catus) are small carnivorous mammals that have been companions to humans for thousands of years. Known for their independence, agility, and unique personalities, they are one of the most popular pets worldwide. Cats have strong hunting instincts, which is why they often chase small moving objects, even when they are well-fed. They communicate through body language, vocalizations like meows and purrs, and scent marking.

Cats can adapt to different living conditions, from rural farms to small city apartments, making them versatile and resilient animals.

Likes

Cats enjoy warm and cozy places where they can rest, such as sunny windowsills or soft blankets. They are playful creatures and like toys that mimic prey, such as feather wands, small balls, and string-like objects. Many cats love to climb or perch in high places where they can observe their surroundings. Gentle petting, especially on the head and chin, is often appreciated. Some cats also enjoy interactive feeding games that allow them to use their hunting skills.

Dislikes

Cats dislike loud noises such as fireworks, vacuum cleaners, or sudden bangs, as these can cause stress. Many are sensitive to strong smells like citrus, vinegar, or certain cleaning chemicals. They usually dislike rough handling, being restrained for too long, or having their tails pulled.

Sudden changes in routine, such as moving houses or altering feeding times, can also make them anxious. While some cats tolerate water, many dislike baths or getting wet.

Habitat

In the wild, cats can be found in diverse habitats, including forests, grasslands, deserts, and urban areas. Their natural adaptability allows them to survive in both warm and cold environments. Domesticated cats need safe shelter, access to fresh water, and a steady food supply.

While many cats live entirely indoors for safety, others are allowed limited outdoor time under supervision or in secure enclosures.

Natural Behaviours

Cats display a variety of natural behaviors that come from their wild ancestors. They are skilled hunters, using stealth and patience to stalk prey. Scratching is used to mark territory and maintain healthy claws. Grooming is an important daily activity for cleanliness and scent maintenance.

Cats purr when content, but also sometimes when anxious or unwell. Kneading with their paws is a comfort behavior often linked to kittenhood. Many cats prefer high vantage points for safety and observation.

Domestication

Cats were first domesticated around 9,000 years ago in the Near East, particularly in farming communities. They were likely attracted to human settlements by the abundance of rodents feeding on stored grain. Humans recognized their usefulness in pest control and allowed them to stay. Unlike dogs, cats largely domesticated themselves by living alongside humans and slowly becoming more social and tolerant over generations.

Reasons You Should Own a Cat

Cats can offer companionship, comfort, and emotional support to their owners. Their playful and curious nature makes them entertaining to watch and interact with. They are relatively independent compared to some pets, meaning they can be left alone for longer periods.

Cats do not require outdoor walks, making them suitable for people with busy lifestyles or small living spaces. Many owners find a cat’s purring and affectionate moments soothing after a stressful day.

Things To Know Before Owning One

Before adopting a cat, consider your lifestyle, time, and financial readiness. Cats require a long-term commitment, often living 12–18 years. You will need to budget for food, litter, toys, grooming supplies, and veterinary care, including vaccinations and possible emergency treatments. They need mental and physical stimulation, so interactive play and environmental enrichment are important. You should also consider whether you can provide a safe environment, especially if you plan to keep the cat indoors only.

Fun Facts

  • Cats have a unique “righting reflex” that allows them to land on their feet most of the time when they fall.
  • Cats can make over 100 different sounds, while dogs make about 10.
  • Their whiskers are highly sensitive and can detect changes in air currents, helping them navigate in the dark.
  • Cats sleep for 12–16 hours a day, making them one of the sleepiest animals.
  • The oldest known pet cat was found in a 9,500-year-old grave in Cyprus, showing cats were domesticated long before ancient Egypt
  • Cats’ nose prints are as unique as human fingerprints.
  • Cats are famously obsessed with boxes because enclosed spaces make them feel safe and reduce stress.
  • Despite their love for milk in cartoons, many adult cats are actually lactose intolerant.
  • Cats often “knead” with their paws because it’s a comforting behavior from kittenhood when they kneaded their mother for milk.
  • When cats bring you “gifts” like dead prey, it’s a sign they see you as part of their family.
